Atmosphere

  2. The ozone layer protects the Earth from harmful ultraviolet radiation from the sun.

Earth history

Earth is estimated to be around 4.54 billion years old, based on radiometric dating of rocks and meteorites. The earliest known life forms on Earth were single-celled organisms that appeared around 3.5 billion years ago, and for much of Earth's history, life remained confined to the oceans.

Over time, life evolved and diversified, with multicellular organisms appearing around 600 million years ago. The first land plants appeared around 450 million years ago, and animals began to colonize the land around 400 million years ago. Humans, the most recent addition to the Earth's biota, evolved around 200,000 years ago and have since had a profound impact on the planet's ecosystems and geology.
